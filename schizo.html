Mizosu's Theory of Perceptual Relativity



Overview


Mizosu's Theory of Perceptual Relativity is a scientific theory that suggests that the universe is made up of an individual's perception of reality. The theory holds that if an individual perceives something, it is real for them, and if they don't, it is not.


The theory is "local" in that it only applies to the individual who is perceiving something and not to others. Reality, according to this theory, is subjective, and everyone's perception of the world is influenced by their own thoughts, beliefs, experiences, and biases.



Examples


  • A person who is hallucinating may see something that others do not see. In their reality, the hallucination is real, but in the reality of others, it is not real because they cannot perceive it.

  • If a schizophrenic person thinks they are talking to God, then for them, God is real, and they are indeed talking to him. However, for others who cannot perceive the conversation, the idea that the person is talking to God may seem delusional.

  • Your views on a specific person make up who that person actually is. There can be two vastly different "copies" of the same person across the realities of two different people.

  • A person's beliefs of the afterlife affect what happens when we die. If you firmly believe you go to Heaven after you die, then you will go to Heaven. If you believe you will be reincarnated, you will be reincarnated.


Comparison to Local Variables


Mizosu's Theory of Perceptual Relativity can be compared to local variables in programming. Just as local variables only have meaning within the scope of the function in which they are declared, a person's perception of reality only has meaning within their own mind. Each person has their own local "universe", which they have control over. This control comes from one's personal thoughts, experiences, and biases.



Conclusion


Mizosu's Theory of Perceptual Relativity provides a new perspective on the nature of reality and the relationship between an individual's perception of the world and their reality. The theory suggests that reality is subjective and that the universe is made up of an individual's perception of it.
