Miz's Basic Guide to Writing the English Language in Egyptian Hieroglyphs


Reading

Hieroglyphs can be read Left to Right, Right to Left, and Top to Bottom. The faces of the Hieroglyphs point towards the side you start at. Any Hieroglyphs that are stacked on top of each other are read from top to bottom. Words are written in the neatest way possible - Hieroglyphs can be both side by side and stacked in one word.


Words written in Hieroglyphs do not contain vowels. Most notably "e" ("eh"). Some vowels are accepted, such as "oo" or "ee".


Sounds

Here is a list of the most commonly used Hieroglyphs with sounds relating directly to the English language. The list contains the Hieroglyph, the sound, and an example of an English word that contains the sound. Note that some English sounds are not present: they were never used, but have substitutions. Some Hieroglyphs represent sounds that would normally take two English letters to pronounce.


  • 𓄿 - The Vulture - "ahh" - "Cat"
  • 𓂝 - The Arm - "ayy" - "Cake"
  • 𓃀 - The Leg - "buh" - "Ball"
  • 𓎡 - The Basket - "kuh" - "Cat"
  • 𓍿 - The Rope - "cha" - "Chicken"
  • 𓂧 - The Hand - "duh" - "Dumbass"
  • 𓇋𓇋 - The Double Reed Leaf - "ee" - "Seek/Mary"
  • 𓆑 - The Horned Viper - "fuh" - "Fuck"
  • 𓎼 - The Jar - "guh" - "Garage"
  • 𓉔 - The Reed Shelter - "hhh" - "Harambe"
  • 𓇋 - The Single Reed Leaf - "ih/yuh" - "Hit / You"
  • 𓆓 - The Cobra - "juh" - "Jar"
  • 𓃭 - The Lion - "lah" - "Ligma"
  • 𓅓 - The Owl - "muh" - "Manjaro"
  • 𓈖 - The Ripple of Water - "nuh" - "Ninja"
  • 𓊪 - The Reed Mat - "puh" - "Pencil"
  • 𓂋 - The Mouth - "rrr" - "Rabbit"
  • 𓋴 - The Folded Cloth - "sss" - "Serpent"
  • 𓈙 - The Lake - "sha" - "Shit / Machine"
  • 𓏏 - The Bread Loaf - "tah" - "Tax"
  • 𓅱 - The Quail Chick - "wuh/ooo" - "Wisdom / Moon"
  • 𓊃 - The Bolt - "zzz" - "Zimbabwe"

Notes

  • The Lion (𓃭 "lah") represented the "L" sound, but was never used in the Egyptian language. It is used solely for foreign names containing the "L" sound, otherwise it was replaced with the Mouth (𓂋 "rrr").
  • Male names are followed by the Seated Man (𓀀), while Female names are followed by a Seated Woman (𓁐).
  • A dash (𓏤) following a Hieroglyph turns that individual Hieroglyph into the object/idea it represents, rather than the letter.
